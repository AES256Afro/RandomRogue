WORLD AND STORY

1,000-year world recipe

Five eras generate regions, factions, wars, plagues, gods, beasts, artifacts, institutions, and named historical figures from one reproducible seed.

1,000 playable scenarios

A Story Director cools recent cards, openings, structures, roles, and themes. Exact cards never repeat within one life.

Remembered campaigns

Fifty-nine connected story families recall your choice, result, location, and witnesses when they return.

Seven ending horizons

Endings read the material history you created. Victories remain partial, unfinished, and available to future people.

PEOPLE, POWER, AND CONSEQUENCE

Living relationships

People track trust, fear, respect, debt, affection, knowledge, grudges, relatives, rivals, employers, and confidants.

Playable institutions

Join unions, councils, cooperatives, labs, peace movements, restoration crews, pirate assemblies, and revolutionary committees.

Material conditions

Worker power, rent, wealth, pollution, militarization, food, fascist influence, solidarity, legitimacy, and mutual aid change through play.

Visible world responses

Major choices print their public consequences and enter the Story Ledger instead of disappearing behind stat changes.

ROGUELIKE SYSTEMS

Places without map chores

Cities, taverns, forests, roads, swamps, caves, dungeons, coasts, mountains, crash sites, and open water each deal different trouble.

Inventory and crafting

Weapons, armor, bags, food, books, recordings, clues, maps, kits, keys, vehicles, relics, and crafted equipment carry history-aware flavor.

Contracts and mysteries

Hunt, deliver, reconcile, survey, investigate, recover artifacts, stock mutual aid, and accuse culprits fixed before the clues.

Naval and spaceship arcs

Cross coasts and open water, bargain with maritime institutions, assemble a vessel, or pursue an ending beyond the chart.

PLAY AND ACCESS

Everywhere you play

Native builds target Windows, Linux, and macOS. The browser build supports desktop, iPad, touch, offline installation, and fullscreen play.

Readable by design

Three reader sizes, high contrast, reduced motion, scrollable long text, large touch targets, and separate music and sound controls.

Persistent and portable

Background autosave, backup recovery, save transfer, heirs, legacies, daily worlds, weekly worlds, and replayable final journeys.

Music starts muted

Sound effects remain available separately. Players decide when the procedural chiptune joins the poor decisions.
