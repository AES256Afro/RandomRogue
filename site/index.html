RANDOM ROGUE

a game of poor decisions

A lofi roguelike where every world simulates 1,000 years of history before you arrive — and when you die, your grave appears in other players' worlds.

The world doesn't wait for you

Before your first step, the recipe runs: one thousand years of geography, factions, wars, artifacts, named beasts, plagues, and a century when things wouldn't stop falling out of the sky. Five ages. Five spoken tongues — goblins sound goblin, swamp folk sound damp, dead corporations sound like liability waivers. Cities burn and become the dungeons you'll loot. Then you're born into the wreckage with six stats, a rusty sword, and, if you choose one, an ambition.

Every card is a choice

2–4 options, visible dice rolls, outcomes that shift with who you are — cursed, famous, wanted, at war, in winter. The same card never plays the same twice.

The Chronicle absorbs you

Die, and you become this world's history: your epitaph in the record, your sword scattered for your next character to find, your heir carrying your name — and your ghost waiting at the spot where you fell.

A shared graveyard

In the daily and weekly worlds, other players' deaths become fresh graves in YOUR world — read their epitaphs, loot their relics, hear their deeds as news, and watch their final run card by card.

You are not the only adventurer

Every world rolls a rival who hunts your bounties and drinks in your taverns. Gods bank your devotion and may catch you at the moment of death — once. The folk you rob remember your heirs.

The sea has an edge

Win a sloop at cards, sail between shores, surf a leviathan's bow-wave — and somewhere in the chart locker is a line drawn off the last page, with a note: the water does not end. we just stopped asking.

Worlds that sharpen

Same seed, same thousand years, on any machine. A world you keep dying in grows thick with your ghosts — and it hits harder and pays better with every life it takes. Send a friend the number.

One card, mid-run

Day 5 · the open water · She crosses your bow in the grey before dawn, sails full on a windless sea: a ship with no wake, crewed by nobody, riding lights burning a color lights don't come in. Her stern-board reads clear as judgment — she was Dozeglub Humidmurr's ship, and Dozeglub Humidmurr has been dead since year 187.

Below the world: the MUD

The Wide World has a basement. Random Rogue MUD is a free multiplayer text dungeon running beneath this game: the same seed-verse, the same gods of doors and debts, twenty-five floors of it, live and shared with other players. Play it in the browser, install the Rogue Deck app, or point any telnet MUD client at it.

The two games keep one ledger. When your run ends up here, your character crosses over: the fallen walk the MUD's halls as ghosts, telling strangers who they were, how many days they lasted, and what their epitaph said. Dungeon crawlers can examine your ghost and learn from your mistakes, which is more than most of us get. Their dead are listed on the MUD's Wall of the Fallen; yours will be too.
